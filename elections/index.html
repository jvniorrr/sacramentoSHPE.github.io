SHPE 2021-2022 Nominees

Executives

Mar Ambriz

Hello my name is Mar and my pronouns are they/them/theirs. I am running for the president position for SHPE this upcoming school year.
I have been a member of SHPE for two years and an officer for one. I have been on both ends of the sword which has given me knowledge on what our members will benefit from the most and how to execute these ideas effectively. Being an officer for one year and shadowing our current president helped me understand what goes on behind the scenes. I have also gained leadership skills being social chair for the Engineering and Computer Science Joint Council (ECsJC) which communicates with the entire student body of our college. Engaging with my peers on a larger scale has facilitated my communication with faculty members such as, the associate dean. This allowed me to be more aware of important club deadlines that were crucial to the conventions a lot of our members were able to attended cost free. I have also attended meetings from other minority organizations such as our wonderful Latinx Council. I was able to gather resources and events from other clubs not necessarily STEM related but important resources specifically for the minority groups we are part of. The latest position I have taken on is being an operations team member for Sac Hacks. I am thankful for being part of such different organizations because they each have given a wider perspective on how to take on such a demanding position.
My plan is to continue all the work the current board has executed throughout these unprecedented times. I would like to continue using virtual platforms (Discord) to ease communication with our members. However, I would encourage all officers to be more interactive on the various channels to facilitate communication even more. Another great positive outcome were the interactions made with other university and professional chapters. I would like to continue nurturing these relationships but also creating new ones to expand our SHPE familia.
Focusing on our SHPE familia will be my number one priority. I plan on executing this by listening to their needs and creating solutions. This year, I came across an alumni who mentioned to me some needs they which would’ve been met while they were in Sac State and part of SHPE. They found that some of our resources were not catered to DACA recipient. Some scenarios are different for DACA students than the average people. I took on the initiative by reaching out organizations that help STEM DACA people and plan on making these resources available for whoever may need them. I want to ensure every member has the opportunity to express their needs and that our team of officers will create new workshops/committees/ meetings/etc that will meet those needs, regardless of their personal or academic background.
As for improving officer communication, I would create 4 different teams among us, mixing returning and new officers. This will allow the new officers to have a direct line of communication to those who have already done this before.
I will not try to improve on what has already been perfected but I will also create new doors of opportunity for our members to go through. Thank you for letting me serve you as treasurer this year and I look forward to having the opportunity to be your president next year.

Wilder Mazariegos

Joshua Rivera

As a junior chapter representative, I would like to make it easy for SHPE members to complete their community service hours, as well as, help the students from the junior chapter to figure out what field of engineering they would like to study. I plan to do this by having at least one event a month. That gives SHPE members plenty of opportunities to get their community service hours completed. As for helping the students from the Junior Chapter I would like to have each event focus on a different field of engineering. When I was in high school, I was struggling to figure out what field of engineering I wanted to go into. It wasn’t until I had done many projects in my engineering class that had helped me decide what I wanted to major in. By having different events, I can give these students some insight as to what each field does. For example, one event can be centered around Arduinos to interest students into computer science or computer engineering. Another event can be centered around building the strongest bridge to get students into civil engineering. I could also ask other SHPE members from different fields what projects relating to their fields would interest students. Overall, as the Junior Chapter Representative I would like to make it easy for current SHPE members to complete their community service hours. I also plan to help students find out what engineering field they are passionate about by having a variety of events be focused on different fields.

About Us

Mission

SHPE changes lives by empowering the Hispanic community to realize its fullest potential and to impact the world through STEM awareness, access, support, and development.

Vision

SHPE’s vision is a world where Hispanics are highly valued and influential as the leading innovators, scientists, mathematicians, and engineers.
